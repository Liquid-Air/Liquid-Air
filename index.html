Supplies needed to build: Copper tubing, Aluminum foil, PVC pipes, Aquarium pump, clear tubing, Aluminum tray, and a small cooler. These materials are just suggestions and any alternatives that are available will still work with the design.

Our Design

We have created an easy-to-make prototype water generator, capable of changing water from the air into clean drinking water.

How to Make It

Step 1 - Tubing

For this step you will need to have some form of metal tubing, like copper or steel. Twist the tubing in a spiral shape about as long as your collection tray.

Step 2 - Cooler

For this step you will need some sort of insulator, like a thermos or styrofoam. Construct as shown in the video, and be sure to make the cooler water-tight.

Step 3 - Cut PVC

For this step you will need a solid material to build a frame, like PVC pipe. Cut two pieces of your material a bit longer than the length of your collection tray, and four pieces twice as tall as your collection tray.

Step 4 - Connect the Frame

Take your frame material and attach them at the corners to form two frame pieces. Place your tubing on the frame pieces so it wraps around the frame.

Step 5 - Build the Foundation

Place your frame above your collection tray, and connect your tubing to the water cooler. Fill the cooler will cool water and turn on the motor to power the machine.

The Science Behind the Design

Our water generator uses theories from the water cycle to achieve its final result. The motor pumps cool water through a system of pipes in the design, making the pipes cooler than the air around them. This causes condensation and clean water to form on the coils and drip into the collection tray, providing the user with clean, fresh water.
